B40 Food Relief Management System

The B40 Food Relief Management System is a comprehensive digital platform built to combat food insecurity by streamlining the management of food donations and distribution to low-income (B40) households. The system is designed to support food banks, donors, and volunteers through the following key features:


  • Inventory Management: Efficiently tracks donated food items, monitors expiry dates, and helps minimize food waste through real-time stock updates.
  • Beneficiary Management: Enables seamless registration and updating of recipient information, ensuring that aid reaches the right individuals at the right time.

By integrating these features, the system improves the transparency and efficiency of food resource allocation, ultimately strengthening the collective effort to reduce hunger and support vulnerable communities.

UTM REC Research Ethics Application System

This project focuses on developing an automated online platform for UTM's Research Ethics Committee (REC) to streamline and modernize the ethics application process. Key features include:

  • Real-time Tracking: Applicants can instantly monitor submission status.
  • Secure Uploads: Easy document submission and centralized storage.
  • Automated Management: Tracks documents, flags missing items, and ensures version control.
  • Deadline Reminders: Alerts users to upcoming tasks and deadlines.
  • In-System Messaging: Facilitates direct communication between applicants and reviewers.

By digitizing and automating the process, the system reduces administrative workload, improves transparency, and accelerates reviews for a more efficient, secure, and user-friendly experience.
